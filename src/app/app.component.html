Top Education-Focused NGOs in India

Pratham

Founded: 1995, Mumbai
Focus: Quality education for underprivileged children
Programs: Foundational learning, vocational training, digital literacy
Notable Work: Publishes the Annual Status of Education Report (ASER)
Reach: Operates across 23+ states in India
Approach: Low-cost, scalable, community-driven models

CRY (Child Rights and You)

Founded: 1979 by Rippan Kapur
Mission: Ensure every child’s right to education, health, and protection
Focus: Education, healthcare, nutrition, protection from exploitation
Reach: Works in 19 states, impacting millions of children
Approach: Grassroots programs, advocacy, and policy change

Akshaya Patra Foundation

Founded: 2000 – ISKCON‑backed NGO
Mission: No child should miss school due to hunger
Work: Runs mid‑day meal programme under PM POSHAN; also Anganwadi feeding, breakfast schemes, and relief during disasters
Reach: Feeds 2.25+ million children daily in 16 states & 3 UTs via 78 kitchens; 4+ billion meals served since inception

Teach For India

Founded: 2009 by Shaheen Mistri
Mission: Provide excellent education to every child in India
Program: 2-year paid fellowship where young professionals teach full-time in low-income schools across cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Pune, Bengaluru, and more
Impact: Over 32,000 students reached directly; thousands of alumni working in education and social impact
Eligibility: Graduates (any stream); no teaching experience needed

Bhumi

Bhumi is one of India’s largest youth-driven NGOs, founded in 2006. It focuses on education for underprivileged children through volunteer-driven programs like teaching English, Math, Science, and life skills on weekends. It also runs environmental and civic initiatives.

Barefoot College

Barefoot College, founded in 1972 in Rajasthan, empowers rural communities, especially women, through education, solar electrification, and skill development. It trains illiterate and semi-literate women—often grandmothers—to become solar engineers, educators, and entrepreneurs.

Deepalaya

Deepalaya, established in 1979 in Delhi, is a non-profit focused on education, health, and empowerment of underprivileged children and communities. It runs schools, vocational training, and programs for differently-abled children and women.

Smile Foundation

Smile Foundation, founded in 2002, is a national-level NGO working on education, healthcare, and livelihood for underprivileged children and families across India. Its flagship program Mission Education has impacted millions of children.

Top Healthcare NGOs in India

Doctors For You

Doctors For You is a humanitarian NGO founded in 2007 that provides emergency medical care, disaster relief, and public health services across India, especially in underserved areas.

Karuna Trust

Karuna Trust is a public health NGO founded in 1986 in Karnataka. It focuses on rural healthcare, managing primary health centers (PHCs) in underserved areas, and works on disease control, mental health, and community development.

SNEHA

SNEHA (Society for Nutrition, Education and Health Action) is a Mumbai-based NGO founded in 1999. It works to improve maternal and child health, prevent gender-based violence, and promote nutrition and healthcare in urban slums.

The Banyan

The Banyan is a Chennai-based NGO founded in 1993 that provides mental health care and rehabilitation for the homeless and marginalized. It runs hospitals, community mental health programs, and inclusive housing solutions.

Smile Foundation

Smile Foundation is an NGO in India (est. 2002) that provides healthcare, education, and support to underprivileged children and families.

Aravind Eye Care

Aravind Eye Care is a non-profit eye care system founded in 1976 in Madurai. It provides high-quality, affordable eye care, especially to the poor, and is one of the largest providers of eye surgeries globally.

Operation ASHA

Operation ASHA is a healthcare NGO founded in 2006 that focuses on tuberculosis (TB) detection, treatment, and awareness among underserved populations using low-cost, technology-enabled solutions.

SEWA

SEWA (Self Employed Women’s Association) is a women’s trade union founded in 1972 in Ahmedabad. It empowers poor, self-employed women by providing access to healthcare, livelihood, childcare, and social security.

About Us

Aashram NGO is a non-profit organization committed to uplifting underprivileged communities. Our mission is to provide education, healthcare, and support to those in need, empowering individuals to build a better future. Through various initiatives and the support of our volunteers and donors, we strive to create lasting positive change.

Our Services

We offer a range of services including free education programs, healthcare camps, vocational training, and community support initiatives. Our dedicated team works tirelessly to ensure that help reaches those who need it most.
